AAM: Managing Infighting / Conflict Resolution for Activists

Every group and relationship can experience conflict. It simply seems like it's part of being human. When you have people with varied personalities and experiences, that can cause friction if something is not agreed upon. This program offers tools to overcome conflict in a group and resolve issues to be an effective activist for the animals.

Security Culture: Staying Safe Online and Offline

DISCLAIMER: This workshop is not intended to encourage illegal activity. It is for educational purposes only. Additionally, the speakers are not attorneys and what they say should not be taken as legal advice. Lastly, be aware that much of the discussion revolves around laws in the United States.



In this workshop Joseph Buddenberg and Jerry Vlasak train animal activists in staying safe both in-person and online. Topics include:

  • Defining Security Culture
  • Exploring possible threats and risks as activists
  • Who and What to avoid
  • Jerry and Joseph share their personal experiences recognizing and handling infiltration
  • Handling Police & FBI visits & interactions
  • Grand Juries: what to do if subpoenaed
  • Tips and strategies for maintaining safety for yourself and your group during activism

AAM: Interacting Effectively with Police as Activists

Disclaimer: The speakers in this workshop are not lawyers and the information in this workshop should not be taken as legal advice.



In this workshop Jonathan Horn & Adam Henry cover:

  • How to talk to the police
  • Dos & Don'ts when interacting with police
  • The Role of a Police Liaison
  • What typically happens post-arrest
  • How to remain professional and protect your communities against repression
